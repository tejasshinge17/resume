About ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the national space agency of India, headquartered in Bengaluru. It operates under the Department of Space (DOS) which is directly overseen by the Prime Minister of India. ISRO was established on August 15, 1969, succeeding the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR), which was established in 1962 by Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, widely regarded as the father of the Indian space program.

ISRO's primary objective is to develop space technology and its application to various national tasks. This includes developing and operationalizing launch vehicles and rocket engines, satellites, and ground systems. ISRO's vision is to "harness space technology for national development while pursuing space science research and planetary exploration."

Over the decades, ISRO has evolved into a world-class space agency, renowned for its cost-effective and reliable missions, contributing significantly to telecommunications, remote sensing, meteorology, and scientific research in India and globally.

Our History

  • 1962: Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR) formed under Dr. Vikram Sarabhai.
  • 1963: First sounding rocket launched from Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station (TERLS).
  • 1969: ISRO established, replacing INCOSPAR.
  • 1975: Launch of Aryabhata, India's first satellite, from a Soviet Cosmodrome.
  • 1980: SLV-3 successfully launches Rohini satellite, making India the sixth nation to achieve orbital launch capability.
  • 1983: INSAT-1B launched, marking a major step in India's communication satellite program.
  • 1993: First successful developmental flight of PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle).
  • 2001: First successful developmental flight of GSLV (Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle).
  • 2008: Chandrayaan-1, India's first mission to the Moon, discovers water molecules on the lunar surface.
  • 2013: Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) launched, successfully entering Mars orbit in 2014.
  • 2017: PSLV-C37 successfully launches 104 satellites in a single mission, a world record.
  • 2019: Chandrayaan-2 mission launched, including an orbiter, lander, and rover.
  • 2023: Chandrayaan-3 successfully soft-lands on the Moon's south polar region.
  • 2023: Aditya-L1, India's first solar observatory mission, launched.

Key Achievements & Milestones

  • Indigenous Launch Vehicle Development: Mastered the technology for PSLV and GSLV, enabling self-reliance in satellite launches.
  • Satellite Constellations: Developed comprehensive satellite systems for communication (INSAT/GSAT), Earth observation (IRS), and navigation (NavIC).
  • Lunar Exploration: Chandrayaan-1 confirmed water on the Moon, and Chandrayaan-3 achieved a historic soft landing near the lunar south pole.
  • Interplanetary Mission: Mangalyaan (Mars Orbiter Mission) made India the first Asian nation to reach Mars orbit on its maiden attempt.
  • Cost-Effective Space Missions: Renowned globally for executing complex space missions at significantly lower costs.
  • International Collaboration: Engaged in numerous international collaborations, including NISAR with NASA.
  • Space-based Applications: Enabled widespread use of space technology for disaster management, urban planning, agriculture, and education.

Future Missions & Endeavors

  • Gaganyaan: India's first human spaceflight mission, aiming to send astronauts into low Earth orbit.
  • Shukrayaan-1: A planned orbiter mission to Venus to study its surface and atmosphere.
  • Lunar Polar Exploration Mission (LUPEX): A joint mission with JAXA (Japan) to explore the Moon's polar regions.
  • NISAR (NASA-ISRO Synthetic Aperture Radar): A joint Earth observation satellite with NASA for advanced remote sensing.
  • Next-Generation Launch Vehicle (NGLV): Development of a heavy-lift, reusable rocket for future space transportation needs.
  • Space Station Module: Long-term plans include developing an Indian space station module.

Leadership & Visionaries

  • Dr. Vikram Sarabhai: The visionary founder of ISRO, whose pioneering efforts laid the foundation for India's space program.
  • Satish Dhawan: Former Chairman of ISRO, under whose leadership the Indian space program achieved significant operational milestones.
  • Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam: Renowned scientist and former President of India, who played a pivotal role in India's missile and space programs.
  • Current Chairman: ISRO has been led by many distinguished scientists and engineers. The current chairman continues to guide the organization towards new frontiers in space exploration and technology.
